Cooldown Management

Core Cooldown Principles

  • Timing over Hoarding: Use cooldowns early and frequently rather than saving them
  • Stack for Impact: Coordinate multiple cooldowns for maximum DPS windows
  • Fight Duration Awareness: Optimize usage based on predicted boss kill times
  • Threat Consideration: Balance maximum DPS with threat management

Major DPS Cooldowns

Death Wish
Cooldown
3 minutes
Duration
30 seconds

Effects:

  • Increases physical damage by 20%
  • Grants Fear immunity for the duration
  • Reduces armor and resistances by 20%

Primary DPS cooldown - should be used early and often throughout encounters.

Recklessness
Duration
15 seconds
Crit Chance
100%

Effects:

  • Guarantees critical hits for all melee attacks
  • Increases damage taken by 20%
  • Generates significant threat

Extremely powerful but requires careful coordination with tanks due to threat generation.

Mighty Rage Potion
Cooldown
2 minutes
Strength Boost
+60 (20s)

Effects:

  • Immediate rage gain of 45-75 points
  • Strength increased by 60 for 20 seconds
  • Best utilized during Execute phase for maximum impact

Essential consumable for maximizing DPS during critical phases.

Utility Cooldowns

Bloodrage
Cooldown
1 minute
Total Rage
20 points

Primary Uses:

  • Battle Shout activation when rage-starved
  • Rage generation mitigation during low-damage phases
  • Execute enhancement for additional damage scaling
  • Pre-pull preparation to ensure ability availability
Berserker Rage
Cooldown
30 seconds
Immunity Duration
10 seconds

Effects:

  • Provides Fear and Incapacitate immunity
  • Generates additional rage when taking damage
  • Essential for encounters with fear mechanics

Strategic Cooldown Usage

Optimal Coordination Strategies:

  • Opening Burst: Death Wish + Recklessness for maximum initial DPS
  • Execute Phase: All cooldowns + Mighty Rage Potion for final 20%
  • Multi-Use Planning: Use Death Wish 2-3 times per encounter based on length
  • Threat Windows: Coordinate with tank cooldowns and aggro drops

Remember: A cooldown saved is a cooldown wasted. Plan usage around encounter timing rather than "perfect moments" that may never come.
