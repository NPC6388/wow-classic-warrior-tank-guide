Vanilla WoW Attack Table Overview

The Attack Table is the server-side mechanism that determines the outcome of melee and ranged attacks in Vanilla World of Warcraft. This system uses a single random number roll to resolve attack results through a strict hierarchy of precedence.

Core Vanilla Mechanics

  • Single Random Roll: Server generates one random number (0-100) per attack
  • Absolute Percentages: All chances are absolute, not relative
  • Mutually Exclusive Results: Only one outcome possible per attack
  • Strict Hierarchy: Results resolved in descending order of precedence

Vanilla Attack Resolution Hierarchy

Attack Results (Descending Order of Precedence):

  1. Miss
  2. Dodge
  3. Parry
  4. Glancing Blow (players/pets vs mobs only)
  5. Block
  6. Critical Hit
  7. Crushing Blow (mobs vs players/pets only)
  8. Ordinary Hit
Vanilla Rule: If the total chances of all results above "Ordinary Hit" equal or exceed 100%, ordinary hits become impossible. This is crucial for tank survivability calculations in Classic WoW.

Vanilla-Specific Mechanics

Attack Result Players vs Mobs Mobs vs Players Notes
Miss Yes Yes Affected by hit rating/weapon skill
Dodge Yes Yes Cannot occur from behind
Parry Yes Yes Cannot occur from behind
Glancing Blow Yes No Reduces damage significantly
Block Yes (with shield) Yes (with shield) Reduces damage, cannot occur from behind
Critical Hit Yes Yes 2x damage
Crushing Blow No Yes 1.5x damage, major threat to tanks

Vanilla Combat Positioning

Positional Attack Mechanics

  • Attacks from Behind: Remove parry and block chances entirely
  • Ranged Attacks: Cannot be dodged or parried
  • Special Attacks: May use different resolution methods (yellow damage)
Example Attack Table Calculation:

Random Roll: 0-100
0-5: Miss (5%)
6-15: Dodge (10%)
16-25: Parry (10%)
26-35: Glancing (10%)
36-45: Block (10%)
46-50: Critical (5%)
51-60: Crushing (10%) [Mob attacking player]
61-100: Normal Hit (40%)

Vanilla Tank Implications

Critical Tank Concepts

  • Crushing Blows: Major threat - 50% extra damage from raid bosses
  • Glancing Blows: Reduce player damage significantly against higher-level mobs
  • Avoidance Cap: Achieving 100%+ avoidance eliminates normal hits
  • Defense Skill: Crucial for reducing crit chance and increasing avoidance
Scenario Effect Tank Priority
Boss 3+ levels higher Crushing blows possible High
Low defense skill Increased crit chance Critical
100%+ avoidance No normal hits Optimal

Practical Vanilla Applications

Understanding the Vanilla Attack Table is essential for:

  • Tank Gearing: Balancing defense, dodge, parry, and block
  • Threat Management: Understanding when abilities can be avoided
  • DPS Optimization: Managing hit cap and weapon skill
  • Raid Strategy: Positioning and cooldown timing
Vanilla Tank Formula:
Total Avoidance = Miss% + Dodge% + Parry% + Block%
Goal: Minimize crushing blows and critical hits
Defense Skill Target: 440+ for raid bosses

Legacy Impact

The Vanilla Attack Table system created some of the most complex and strategic tanking gameplay in WoW's history. The constant threat of crushing blows and the need to balance multiple avoidance statistics made tank gearing and positioning critically important.

Classic Era Note: This system remains unchanged in WoW Classic, making this information directly applicable to current Classic Era and Season of Discovery gameplay.
