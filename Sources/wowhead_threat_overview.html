Threat Generation Basics

Threat is the underlying mechanic that determines which player a mob will attack. Understanding threat generation is crucial for successful group and raid encounters.

  • Damage generates threat at a 1:1 ratio - 100 damage = 100 threat
  • Healing generates threat at a 1:0.25 ratio - 100 healing = 25 threat (no overhealing)
  • Resource gains generate threat differently:
    • Mana: 1:0.5 ratio (100 mana = 50 threat)
    • Rage: 5:1 ratio (50 rage = 250 threat, static unmodified)
    • Energy: 5:1 ratio

Warrior Threat Mechanics

Warriors have unique threat generation mechanics based on their stance:

  • Defensive Stance: 130% base threat (149.5% with 5/5 Defiance talent)
  • Battle/Berserker Stances: 80% base threat

Warrior Threat-Generating Abilities

Ability Base Threat Notes
Sunder Armor (Rank 5) 260 threat Stacks up to 5 times, reduces armor
Shield Slam (Rank 4) 250 threat Requires shield, dispels magic effect
Revenge (Rank 5) 315 threat Only usable after dodge/parry/block
Heroic Strike (Rank 9) 175 threat Next melee attack, prevents dual-wield miss

Threat Reduction Modifiers

Several abilities and effects can reduce threat generation:

  • Blessing of Salvation (Paladin): Reduces all threat by 30%
  • Tranquil Air Totem (Shaman): Reduces party threat by 20%
  • Feint (Rogue): Reduces threat temporarily
  • Fade (Priest): Reduces threat temporarily
Key Principle: "Threat is a damage ceiling - the higher a tank's TPS is, the higher a party/raid's DPS can be."

Threat Management Strategy

  • Tanks should focus on maximizing threat per second (TPS)
  • DPS players need to monitor their threat relative to the tank
  • Melee DPS need 110% of tank threat to pull aggro
  • Ranged DPS need 130% of tank threat to pull aggro
  • Use threat reduction abilities when approaching the threat ceiling
